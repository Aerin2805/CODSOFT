Ajit Doval

Indian Intelligence Officer and Diplomat

Early Life and Education

Ajit Doval was born on January 20, 1945, in Ghiri Banelsyun village, Pauri Garhwal district, Uttarakhand, India.

Intelligence and Security Career

Ajit Doval had a distinguished career in the Indian Police Service (IPS) before joining the Research and Analysis Wing (RAW), India's external intelligence agency.

Operation Black Thunder

One of the defining moments in Ajit Doval's career was his leadership in "Operation Black Thunder" in 1988.

Kashmir Conflict

Ajit Doval played a vital role in dealing with the insurgency in Jammu and Kashmir during his tenure as the Director of the Intelligence Bureau (IB).

Kandahar Hijacking Crisis

In 1999, during the Kandahar hijacking crisis, Ajit Doval was a key negotiator and played an instrumental role in securing the release of Indian Airlines Flight 814 passengers held hostage by terrorists.

National Security Advisor (NSA)

In May 2014, Ajit Doval was appointed as India's National Security Advisor (NSA) by Prime Minister Narendra Modi.

Strategic Diplomacy

Ajit Doval's tenure as NSA witnessed a significant shift in India's approach towards strategic diplomacy.

Balochistan and Cross-Border Operations

Under Ajit Doval's leadership, India adopted a more assertive stance in dealing with cross-border terrorism and threats emanating from Pakistan.
